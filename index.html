Bucklew v. Precythe

A landmark Supreme Court case that clarified death penalty standards

1996

Russell Bucklew murdered his ex-girlfriend's new partner. He was arrested and charged with first-degree murder.

1997

Bucklew was sentenced to death in Missouri.

2009

Bucklew was diagnosed with cavernous hemangioma, a rare medical condition that caused blood-filled tumors in his head and neck. He claimed that lethal injection would cause the tumors to rupture, resulting in extreme pain.

2014

Bucklew filed a federal lawsuit arguing that executing him by lethal injection would cause excruciating pain due to his condition, and would therefore violate the Eighth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, which prohibits cruel and unusual punishment.

2018

The U.S. Supreme Court agreed to hear the case, titled Bucklew v. Precythe.

April 1, 2019

In a 5-4 decision, the Supreme Court ruled against Bucklew, holding that he failed to propose a feasible and less painful alternative method of execution. The Court upheld Missouri's lethal injection protocol.

October 1, 2019

Russell Bucklew was executed by lethal injection, ending over two decades of legal appeals.

Historical Significance

  • Narrowed the Scope of the Eighth Amendment
  • Reinforced the Glossip v. Gross (2015) Standard
  • Limited Challenges to Execution Methods
